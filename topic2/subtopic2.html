Equality and Discrimination

Women's experience during colonial America.


Colonial America is the era where women faced different experiences. They were discriminated against, no freedom and/or less rights compared to women's and children today. Everything started when the first European women came to the Southern colonies. It began here because it was headed by a man who presided over his family and business while his wife only worked inside the home.

As the Southern colonies became more established, society reverted to the European model, and white women began focusing on running the household, and managing servants and those they had enslaved. Women can only do household chores, sewing, educating the children, producing soup, and many more. Aside from that, they can also sometimes help their husbands' careers like tavern owners, businessmen, or tradesmen. However, they are still not allowed to vote who they want and no rights to share their opinions. In addition, children are also victim of discrimination before. If a child is born rich or privileged, they can learn etiquette, manners, hosting guests and so on, while a child is born in a lower class, they need to help their mother produce soup, and so on.
